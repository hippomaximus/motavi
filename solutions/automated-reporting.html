The weekly spreadsheet tax

Recurring reports are pure overhead. Someone logs into four dashboards, exports each one, reconciles the numbers, drops them into a template, writes a short summary, and sends it around. It happens every week, takes hours, and produces nothing the data didn't already contain. Worse, because it's manual, it's late, inconsistent, and prone to the kind of copy error that sends leadership the wrong figure.

What automated reporting replaces it with

A reporting workflow in n8n runs on a schedule — every morning, every Monday, the first of the month — and does the whole job without anyone touching it:

  • Gather — pull figures from Google Analytics, your CRM, your store, ad platforms, and SQL databases in one orchestrated run.
  • Aggregate and format — combine the sources, calculate the metrics that matter, and lay them out as a clean table, a PDF, or chart-ready data.
  • Deliver — send it to Slack, email, cloud storage, or straight into a live dashboard, addressed to the people who need it.
  • Alert on anomalies — flag the moment a number crosses a threshold: revenue dips, traffic spikes, a conversion rate falls — so you hear about it when it happens, not in next week's report.

The anomaly alerting is often the highest-value piece. A scheduled report tells you what happened; a threshold alert tells you the moment something needs attention — that's the difference between reacting in an hour and finding out in a week.

Reports we commonly automate

Daily revenue and orders to a Slack channel. Weekly marketing performance pulling spend, leads, and conversion from every channel into one view. Monthly board-ready summaries combining finance and operations data. Inventory-threshold alerts that fire before you run out of stock. Each one turns a recurring manual chore into a pipeline that just runs — reliably, on time, every time.

Frequently asked questions

Where can automated reports be delivered?

Slack, email, Google Drive or other cloud storage, or directly into a live dashboard. The same workflow can deliver to several destinations at once.

Can the workflow alert me when something goes wrong?

Yes. Beyond scheduled reports, we set threshold-based alerts so you're notified the instant a metric crosses a line you define — no waiting for the next report.

What data sources can you pull from?

Google Analytics, CRMs, ad platforms, e-commerce stores, SQL databases, and most tools with an API — combined into a single report.
