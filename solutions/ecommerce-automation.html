E-commerce automation

Your store runs the busywork itself.

Running a store means a hundred small operational tasks that eat the day: chasing stock levels, flagging big orders, recovering abandoned carts, following up after purchase. We build n8n workflows that handle them automatically, wired into Shopify, WooCommerce, and LINE.

The operational drag behind every store

The storefront is the easy part. What wears teams down is everything behind it: watching inventory so you don't oversell, noticing the high-value order that needs a personal touch, winning back the cart someone abandoned at checkout, and keeping customers warm after they buy. Done by hand, these are either neglected or they consume a person's whole day. Done with automation, they run silently and lift revenue while they do.

The flows that move the needle

  • Order alerts — high-value or flagged orders ping your team instantly via Telegram or Slack, so VIP customers get a fast, personal response.
  • Inventory thresholds — when stock drops below a level you set, the workflow alerts you or auto-creates a reorder, so you never silently run out or oversell.
  • Abandoned-cart recovery — a checkout that stalls triggers a timed, personalized follow-up sequence that recovers revenue that would otherwise vanish.
  • Post-purchase sequences — delivery updates, review requests, replenishment reminders, and cross-sells fire automatically at the right moment after the order.
  • Multi-channel sync — orders, customers, and inventory stay consistent across your store, your database, and your fulfilment and marketing tools.

For stores selling through LINE, WhatsApp, or social as well as a web storefront, n8n unifies the channels into one pipeline — so an order is an order no matter where it came from, and the same automations apply to all of them.

Built on a stack we use ourselves

We run e-commerce operations on this exact pattern across our own brands — order capture, inventory alerting, post-purchase flows, and intelligence feeds — on self-hosted n8n with a Postgres backbone. That means the workflows we build for your store aren't theory; they're the same architecture keeping our own shelves stocked and our own customers followed up with. Start with the single flow that's hurting most, then expand into a system that runs the whole operation.

Frequently asked questions

Which e-commerce platforms do you support?

Shopify, WooCommerce, and custom storefronts, plus social and messaging channels like LINE and WhatsApp — unified into one automation pipeline.

Can you recover abandoned carts without a paid app?

Yes. A self-hosted n8n workflow can detect a stalled checkout and trigger a personalized follow-up sequence, with no per-message app fee.

Will inventory stay accurate across channels?

The workflow keeps stock levels synced between your store, database, and fulfilment tools, and alerts you before you run low — so you don't oversell.
